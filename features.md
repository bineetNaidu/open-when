## The Big Misconception (address this first, always)

**Your letters are completely private.**

When you seal your letters, nothing gets saved to a server, a database, or any website.
The letters live entirely inside the link itself.

Think of it like this — the link *is* the letters. Whoever you share that link with can read them. Nobody else. Not us. Not strangers. Nobody who hasn't received that exact link from you.

If you lose the link, the letters are gone. There's no account to log back into.
That's what makes it feel like a real handwritten letter — it only exists for the person holding it.

---

## How It Works — Step by Step

### Step 1 — You write the letters

You sit down and write as many letters as you want. Each one has a title — something like:

- *"Open when you miss me"*
- *"Open when you can't sleep"*
- *"Open when today feels heavy"*
- *"Open when you achieve something big"*

You write the message, pick an emoji, and move on to the next one.
Nobody sees any of this yet. It all stays in your browser until you're ready.

---

### Step 2 — You seal them

When you're done writing, you tap **"Seal Our Letters."**

This is the moment everything gets bundled together — your name, all your letters — and compressed into a single private link. No account needed. No sign-up. It happens instantly.

You'll see a small animation: the letters fold, slide into an envelope, and a wax seal appears.
That seal means they're ready.

---

### Step 3 — You share the link

Copy the link and send it to the person you wrote for.

Send it however you like — a text message, an email, a DM.
The link looks long and messy. That's normal. All your letters are compressed inside it.

**Important:** only share this link with the person it's meant for.
Anyone who receives this link can open the letters inside it.

---

### Step 4 — They open one letter each day

When they open the link, they'll see a sealed envelope waiting for them.

They tap it. It opens. One letter slides out — written by you, meant for exactly this moment.

Tomorrow, they come back to the same link. A different letter opens.
The day after that, another one.

The letters rotate automatically every day. No app to download. No account to create.
They just open the link.

---

### Step 5 — They never see all the letters at once

This is intentional.

They don't get to flip through everything you wrote. They get one letter per day, chosen by the date. The same link, on the same day, always shows the same letter — so if they come back later that day, or share the link with you to show you what they received, it will still be the same letter.

The next letter only unlocks the next day.

## The Questions Everyone Asks

**Can anyone find my letters by searching online?**
No. There's no database. There's no search index. Your letters don't exist anywhere except inside that link. Nobody can find them unless you give them the link.

**What if I send it to the wrong person by accident?**
The letters will be readable by whoever opens that link. There's no way to revoke access once the link is shared. If this happens, go back to the create page and make a new link — the old one will still work, but you can stop sharing it.

**Can I edit the letters after I've shared the link?**
Not directly. Once the link is created, it's fixed. If you want to change something, go back to the create page, rewrite your letters, and seal a new link. Share the new one.

**What if I only write one letter?**
That's completely fine. They'll see the same letter every day. If you want the experience to feel more alive, writing 7 or more letters means a full week of different openings.

**Does the link expire?**
No. The link works forever — as long as the letters inside it haven't been corrupted or the URL hasn't been broken. We recommend they save it somewhere safe, like a notes app or a bookmark.

**What does "one letter per day" actually mean?**
Every day at midnight (on their device's clock), a new letter becomes available. The rotation is based on the date and the specific letters in their link — so it's the same letter for everyone who opens that exact link on that exact day.

**Can they read ahead?**
No. The app is designed to reveal one letter per day. The order isn't something they can skip through. Part of the magic is not knowing what tomorrow's letter says.

**Is this really free?**
Yes. There's no subscription, no premium tier, no sign-up. It costs nothing because nothing is stored — the whole thing runs in the browser.
